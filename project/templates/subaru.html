Subaru

  • Subaru (スバル, /ˈsuːbəruː/ or /sʊˈbɑːruː/;[4][5] Japanese pronunciation: [sɯꜜbaɾɯ][6]) is the automobile manufacturing division of Japanese transportation conglomerate Subaru Corporation (formerly known as Fuji Heavy Industries), the twenty-first largest automaker by production worldwide in 2017.
  • Subaru cars are known for their use of a boxer engine layout in most vehicles above 1,500 cc. The Symmetrical All Wheel Drive drive-train layout was introduced in 1972. Both became standard equipment for mid-size and smaller cars in most markets by 1996. The lone exception is the BRZ, introduced in 2012 via a partnership with Toyota, which pairs the boxer engine with rear-wheel-drive.
  • Subaru is the direct translation from Japanese for the Pleiades star cluster M45, or the "Seven Sisters" (one of whom tradition says is invisible – hence only six stars in the Subaru logo), which in turn inspires the logo and alludes to the companies that merged to create FHI.
  • Subaru also offers turbocharged versions of their passenger cars, such as the WRX, Levorg sti, Outback XT, Ascent, and formerly the Legacy GT, Legacy XT, and Forester XT.
  • Subaru participates in various motorsport events, including rally, rallycross, and hillclimbs. They also compete in the Super Taikyu Series in Japan. In the United States, Subaru Motorsports USA focuses on stage rally, rallycross, record attempts, and desert racing.
