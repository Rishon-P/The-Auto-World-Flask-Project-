Dodge

  • Dodge is an American brand of automobiles and a division of Stellantis North America, based in Auburn Hills, Michigan.
  • Founded as the Dodge Brothers Company machine shop by brothers Horace Elgin Dodge and John Francis Dodge in the early 1900s, Dodge was originally a supplier of parts and assemblies to Detroit-based automakers like Ford. They began building complete automobiles under the "Dodge Brothers" brand in 1914, predating the founding of the Chrysler Corporation.
  • Dodge vehicles have historically included performance cars, and for much of its existence, Dodge was Chrysler's mid-priced brand above Plymouth.
  • Dodge's mainstay vehicles were trucks, full-sized passenger cars through the 1970s, and it also built compact cars such as the 1963 through 1976 Dart and midsize as well as such as the "B-Body" Coronet and Charger from 1965 until 1978.
  • Dodge was acquired by Chrysler Corporation in 1928 . Finally , Dodge became part of Stellantis as a result of the merger between Fiat Chrysler Automobiles (FCA) and the PSA Group on January 16, 2021.
  • From the early 2000s, Dodge's highest-performing vehicles were marketed under Street & Racing Technology (SRT) branding. These models often came equipped with high-performance V8s. These models included the Dodge Challenger SRT, Dodge Charger SRT, Dodge Magnum SRT, Dodge Durango SRT, Dodge Neon SRT-4, Dodge Caliber SRT-4, Dodge Viper, and Dodge Ram SRT-10.
  • In 2015 , FCA launched the Hellcat and Challenger which are powered by hemi V8 .
